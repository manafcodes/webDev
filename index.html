Welcome to Your New Life

Becoming a Muslim is one of the most profound decisions you will ever make. Know that every sin you committed before is forgiven — you start with a completely clean slate. This website was built to walk beside you on this beautiful path. Take your time, ask questions, and remember: Islam is not about perfection, it's about sincerity and progress.

First Steps

Getting Started

These are the most important things to focus on in your first weeks and months as a Muslim.

Step 1

Understand the Shahada

The declaration of faith: "Ashhadu an la ilaha illa Allah..." I bear witness there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger.

Step 2

Learn How to Pray (Salah)

Salah is the second pillar and your direct connection to Allah. Start by learning the movements and the Arabic recitations.

Step 3

Begin Reading the Quran

Start with Surah Al-Fatiha (the opening chapter) and short surahs at the end of the Quran. Read a translation alongside the Arabic.

Step 4

Find a Muslim Community

Visit a local mosque, join online communities, and connect with other Muslims. Islam is not meant to be practiced in isolation.

Step 5

Learn Daily Duas

Duas are supplications — your personal conversations with Allah. Start with simple ones: waking up, eating, sleeping, entering the mosque.

Step 6

Build Islamic Character

Islam is about embodying qualities like honesty, patience, gratitude, kindness, and humility. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "The best among you are those who have the best character."

Beliefs

Six Articles of Faith (Iman)

These are the six core beliefs that every Muslim must hold in their heart.

1

Belief in Allah

Allah is the One God — the Creator, Sustainer, and Judge of all existence. He has no partners, no children, and no equal. He is the Most Merciful, the Most Wise, and the Most Just.

2

Belief in Angels

Angels (Mala'ika) are created from light and carry out Allah's commands. They have no free will. Jibreel (Gabriel) brought revelations, Mikail manages provisions, Israfil will blow the trumpet.

3

Divine Books

Allah revealed scriptures: the Torah to Musa, Psalms to Dawud, Gospel to Isa, and the Quran to Muhammad ﷺ. The Quran is the final, preserved, and unaltered revelation.

4

Belief in Prophets

Allah sent prophets to guide every nation — from Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, and Isa, to the final messenger, Muhammad ﷺ. All taught the same core message: worship Allah alone.

5

Day of Judgment

Every soul will be resurrected and held accountable. A scale will weigh good and bad deeds. Ultimately, Allah's mercy determines where each soul spends eternity.

6

Divine Decree (Qadar)

Allah has knowledge of everything past, present, and future. What He wills happens. This doesn't negate human free will — we choose our actions, but Allah knows what we'll choose.

Essential Practice

How to Pray (Salah)

The prayer is the backbone of a Muslim's day. Here's a simplified breakdown of the five daily prayers.

Steps of the Prayer

Step 1 Intention & Takbeer
Make the intention in your heart. Face the Qiblah. Raise both hands to your ears and say "Allahu Akbar" (Allah is the Greatest). Place your right hand over your left on your chest.
Step 2 Opening Recitation
Recite: "Subhanaka Allahumma wa bihamdika, wa tabarakasmuka..."

Then say "A'udhu billahi minash-Shaytanir-rajeem" (I seek refuge in Allah from Satan) and "Bismillahir-Rahmanir-Raheem" (In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful).
Step 3 Surah Al-Fatiha

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿١﴾ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ﴿٢﴾ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿٣﴾ مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ ﴿٤﴾ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ ﴿٥﴾ اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ ﴿٦﴾ صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ ﴿٧﴾

Translation: In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. All praise is due to Allah, Lord of the worlds. The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Master of the Day of Judgment. You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help. Guide us on the Straight Path — the path of those You have blessed, not of those who incurred Your wrath, nor of those who have gone astray. (Ameen)

Step 4 Ruku (Bowing)
Say "Allahu Akbar" and bow down, placing your hands on your knees with your back straight. In ruku, say "Subhana Rabbiyal-Azeem" (Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great) at least 3 times. Then stand up straight saying "Sami'Allahu liman hamidah".
Step 5 Sujud (Prostration)
Say "Allahu Akbar" and prostrate. In sujud, say "Subhana Rabbiyal-A'la" (Glory be to my Lord, the Most High) at least 3 times. This is the closest position to Allah — make dua here freely. Then sit up briefly, and prostrate a second time.

Clarity

Common Misconceptions

Let's address some of the most widespread misunderstandings about Islam.

Myth

"Islam was spread by the sword"

Reality

The Quran clearly states: "There is no compulsion in religion" (2:256). Historically, Islam spread primarily through trade, migration, and the example of Muslim communities. Many regions embraced Islam without any military conquest.

Myth

"Muslims worship a different God"

Reality

Allah is simply the Arabic word for "God" — the same God of Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and all the prophets. Arab Christians also call God "Allah." Islam teaches that there is only one God, the Creator of everything.

Myth

"Islam oppresses women"

Reality

Islam granted women the right to inherit, own property, initiate divorce, and choose their husbands 1,400 years ago. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The best of you are those who are best to their women." Cultural practices are often not Islamic teachings.

Myth

"Muslims don't believe in Jesus"

Reality

Muslims deeply revere Jesus (Isa) as one of the greatest prophets of Islam. He is mentioned by name 25 times in the Quran. Muslims believe in his virgin birth, his miracles, and his second coming — but not that he is God.

Sacred Times

Key Islamic Dates & Events

The Islamic calendar is lunar (354 days), so dates shift each year by about 11 days.

🌙

Ramadan

9th Month

The holy month of fasting. Muslims abstain from food, drink, and worldly desires from dawn to sunset.

🎉

Eid al-Fitr

1st Shawwal

The "Festival of Breaking the Fast" — a joyous celebration marking the end of Ramadan.

🕋

Hajj

Dhul Hijjah

The annual pilgrimage to Mecca — one of the five pillars. Over 2 million Muslims gather to perform rituals.

🐑

Eid al-Adha

10th Dhul Hijjah

The "Festival of Sacrifice" — performed alongside Hajj, commemorating Ibrahim's devotion to Allah.
